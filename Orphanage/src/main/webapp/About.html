About Orphans Situation

Many orphan children in India are also at risk of malnutrition or are already suffering from it given that most orphans are not in shelters and orphanages.
Most orphan children in India do not have access to necessities like food, shelter, and clean water, which also leads to a lack of education.
Another major problem in India is access to healthcare services, specifically for orphans.
With an almost equal number of couples who want a child and children who need a home, you'd think that adoption rates would be much higher, but due to the complicated adoption process and skewed views of adoption as a whole, most children are left to fend for themselves.
With so few children actually in care homes, most orphans are not available for legal adoption.
There have been reports of parents attempting to sell their children because they can no longer provide for them.
